DRAGON CHICKEN

Restaurant style Dragon Chicken is a spicy, addictive 
Indo Chinese appetizer that takes under 30 minutes to put
together! Crunchy cashews, fried strips of chicken, 
lots of onion and capsicum tossed in a spicy 
red chilli sauce make this a crowd favourite!

Imagine a fire breathing dragon. Now imagine the same when you eat this dish. Haha okay I'm exaggerating - its not that spicy! But seriously, favourite order everytime we eat Chinese outside! And here's why I love about Dragon Chicken so much

INCREDIENTS FOR DRAGON CHICKEN


  • Chicken Thigh
  • Egg White
  • Binding Agents
  • Seasoning: Soy sauce, red chilli powder or cayenne, pepper, salt.
  • Aromatics: Finely chopped ginger and garlic - lots of it.
  • Veggies: Onions and capsicum that are cubed.
  • Cashew nuts
  • Sauces: Red Chilli sauce, soya sauce, ketchup
  • Cornflour

STEP BY STEP RECIPE


  1. Marinate: Marinate the chicken in a mixture of soy sauce, salt, pepper, red chilli powder (or cayenne), rice flour, all purpose flour and egg white. Set this aside while you prep everything else
  2. Fry: Fry the chicken in about 1 inch oil till crispy and brown on both sides. About 2-3 minutes total
  3. Saute aromatics: In hot oil in a wok. There's ginger, garlic and red chilli paste. Saute for 30-45 seconds till the chilli paste is fried and doesn't smell raw anymore
  4. Saute veggies: Onion and capsicum for a minute on high heat till they start turning translucent but still remain crispy
  5. Saute cashews: Saute cashews for 10-15 seconds just to roast them off. I'm using whole cashew nuts. Peanuts would also be amazing here
  6. Add sauces: soy sauce, ketchup, vinegar, salt and pepper
  7. Stir fry Stir fry for 10-15 seconds to get everything mixed well
  8. Thicken with a cornflour slurry basically cornflour mixed with water. Stir fry for another 15 seconds till you see a thick sauce forming. The sauce should be like glaze because this is a dragon chicken dry and we want the sauce to just coat the chicken
  9. Add fried Chicken to the wok right before serving: The longer the chicken sits in the sauces, the softer it'll get
  10. Toss and serve immediately: Top with some spring onions. This doesn't need anything else!

This recipe is generally served as an appetizer, but if you are into it, you can serve this as a dry-ish main dish with some egg fried rice. It's fantastic both ways! And so easily customizable
